# Automated Settlement and Distribution Architecture

## Introduction
The Tactical Trend Trader (3T) project aims to implement an automated settlement and distribution architecture to handle charitable contributions and infrastructure costs. This document outlines the proposed architecture and procedures for achieving this goal.

## Components

* **Beneficiary Management**: A system to manage beneficiary information, including Peter's Pence (Obolo di San Pietro).
* **Contribution Processing**: An automated system to process contributions, including multi-currency banking and blockchain-based ledgers.
* **Settlement Layer**: Integration with settlement layers, such as Stellar, to facilitate transparent and auditable contributions.
* **KYC/AML Compliance**: Implementation of Know Your Customer (KYC) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) tools, regulatory reporting, and compliance protocols.

## Procedures

1. **Draw Down**: Prior to dispersal of generated funding, the system will draw down expenses and starting capital.
2. **Contribution Allocation**: Contributions will be allocated to beneficiaries, such as Peter's Pence, based on predetermined criteria.
3. **Settlement**: Contributions will be settled using the chosen settlement layer, ensuring transparency and auditability.
4. **Reporting**: Regular reporting will be generated to track contributions, expenses, and beneficiary allocations.

## Blockchain Integration
The system will utilize blockchain technology to ensure transparency and auditability of contributions. The Stellar network will be used as the primary settlement layer, leveraging its built-in compliance features, including AML/KYC tools and regulatory reporting.

## KYC Documents
KYC documents will be shared with compliant anchors, such as Keeta, to ensure secure and regulated identity verification.
