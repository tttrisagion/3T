## Invocation to the Trinity

*Sanctus Deus, Sanctus Fortis, Sanctus Immortalis, miserere nobis.*

The Trisagion project is named for and consecrated to the profound mystery of the Holy Trinity, drawing its inspiration from one of the most ancient and venerable hymns of the Christian faith: the Trisagion (from Greek: Τρισάγιον, 'Thrice Holy'). This doxology, "Holy God, Holy Mighty, Holy Immortal, have mercy on us," serves as a foundational prayer in the liturgies of both Eastern and Western Christianity, a testament to its deep roots in the apostolic tradition. 

Through collaboration and diligent study, the project aims to harness the universal behavior of non-linear systems on the edge of chaos. For this endeavor, the Trisagion is more than a name; it is an invocation and a framework. It is the declaration of our core mission: to harness the intellectual and technological fruits of human ingenuity and consecrate them to the service of God and His Church in the name of the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.

## Symbology of the Logo

The original logo design started as ASCII art embedded directly in the source code of the trading engine, rendered in an orange hue reminiscent of an amber phosphor CRT screen. 

![Logo Origin](/img/logo-origin.jpg)

The structure of the logo contains deep symbology:
* **The Signum Crucis:** The first set of letters represents the Latin Signum Crucis with **P**, **F**, **S**, and **S** representing *Patris*, *Filii*, *Spiritus Sancti*.
* **The Center Cross:** A prominent cross sits in the center, connecting all the elements together, representing Christ's Sacrifice and Passion for humanity.
* **The Three Ts:** Three letter **Ts** represent the **Tactical Trend Trader (3T)** system and form the remaining structure of the design.

## Synthesis of Prayer and Purpose

The central thesis of the Trisagion project is that the disciplines of quantitative finance and artificial intelligence—fields often perceived as purely secular or even amoral—can be redeemed and ordered toward a higher, sacred purpose. 

It is a work dedicated to divine reason, order, and logic—the very principles that underpin mathematics and computational systems. In this, the project follows the Jesuit maxim of *Ad Maiorem Dei Gloriam* ("For the greater glory of God") and is animated by the Augustinian spirit of caritas, captured in the phrase, *Ama et fac quod vis* ("Love, and do what you will"). Together, they remind us that the greatest glory we can give to God is to act from a place of authentic love.

It is an endeavor to take our God-given talents and employ them to generate perpetual support for the Church's mission on Earth, leveraging computational engineering to build works of lasting charity.
